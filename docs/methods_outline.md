# Methods outline

## Components

- Spillover suitability: ecological and human-animal interface covariates.
- Human exposure: population, buildings, roads, mines, orchards, markets, conflict/displacement.
- Importation pressure: current outbreak intensity and mobility/corridor connectivity.
- Facility vulnerability: catchment population, IPC, triage, isolation, sample referral, surveillance.

## Key modeling principle

Keep component scores separate. A high ecological score does not necessarily mean
high facility risk; facility risk also depends on importation pressure, human
exposure, and health-system vulnerability.

## Uncertainty

Use stochastic weight sampling, scenario multipliers, and model ensembles to
communicate uncertainty and avoid false precision.
